LEMMS demosaic

1. LEMMS

Suppose X and Y are scalar random variables, and we wish to use the (assumed known)value of Y to estimate the (unknown) value of X. The minimum mean-suqare error(MMSE) estimate is diffcult to estimate. Because of the limited information about X and Y. LMMSE estimation is useful in these cases since it only requires second-order statistics.

2. LEMMS Denosing

$$ f_n = u_n-\alpha_n $$
$$ s = fh $$
suppose $u_n$ is one-dimensional signal, assuming the noise $\alpha$ has low frequency content, and $h$ is a low filter,
Since $u$ is assumed to be smooth, the lowpadd filter signal s should be closer than $f$ to u. *Statistics** of $s$ and $(f-s)$ can be used to estimate statistic of $u$ and $\alpha$
